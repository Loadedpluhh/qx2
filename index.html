Trading is the act of buying and selling financial instruments to profit from price movements. Before risking a single dollar, understand these fundamentals.

How Markets Work

Every trade requires a buyer AND a seller. When you buy EUR/USD, someone else is selling it to you. The price moves based on the imbalance between buyers (demand) and sellers (supply).

The Basic Equation:
More buyers than sellers → Price goes UP ↑
More sellers than buyers → Price goes DOWN ↓
Equal pressure → Price consolidates sideways ↔

Your job as a trader is to identify when this balance is about to shift and position yourself accordingly.

"The goal of a successful trader is to make the best trades. Money is secondary."
— Alexander Elder, Trading for a Living

Choose Your Arena
Financial Markets Explained

Different markets have different characteristics, costs, and hours. Pick ONE to master before diversifying.

💱
Forex (FX)
Trade currency pairs like EUR/USD. $6+ trillion daily volume. Open 24/5. Low barriers to entry, high leverage available (dangerous). Best for: beginners with small accounts.
KEY STATS:
📍 Min account: $100-500 ⏰ Hours: Sun 5pm - Fri 5pm ET 📈 Typical leverage: 50:1 - 500:1 💰 Costs: Spread + swap
📈
Stocks & ETFs
Buy shares of companies (AAPL, TSLA) or baskets (SPY, QQQ). Ownership stake in businesses. Best for: building wealth, swing trading, longer holds.
KEY STATS:
📍 Min account: $0 (fractional shares) ⏰ Hours: 9:30am - 4pm ET 📈 Leverage: 2:1 (margin account) 💰 Costs: Commission + spread
🔮
Futures
Contracts to buy/sell at future date. ES (S&P 500), NQ (Nasdaq), CL (Oil). Standardized, exchange-traded. Best for: experienced traders, large accounts.
KEY STATS:
📍 Min account: $5,000-25,000 ⏰ Hours: Nearly 24/5 📈 Leverage: 20:1 - 50:1 💰 Costs: Commission + exchange fees
⚡
Cryptocurrency
Digital assets (BTC, ETH). 24/7 trading, extreme volatility. Largely unregulated. Best for: high risk tolerance, tech-savvy traders.
KEY STATS:
📍 Min account: $10+ ⏰ Hours: 24/7/365 📈 Leverage: 1:1 - 125:1 💰 Costs: Spread + trading fees
📋
Options
Contracts giving right (not obligation) to buy/sell. Complex Greeks, time decay. Best for: experienced traders, hedging, income strategies.
KEY STATS:
📍 Min account: $2,000+ ⏰ Hours: Market hours 📈 Leverage: Built-in (premium) 💰 Costs: Commission + spread
🏦
CFDs
Contracts for Difference. Trade price movements without owning asset. Available on forex, stocks, indices. NOT available in US. Best for: non-US traders.
KEY STATS:
📍 Min account: $100-500 ⏰ Hours: Varies by instrument 📈 Leverage: 2:1 - 30:1 (EU regulated) 💰 Costs: Spread + overnight fees

Step By Step
Your First 30 Days in Trading

Follow this exact sequence. Skipping steps is why 90% of traders fail within their first year.

Days 1-3: Education Foundation
Read everything on this page. Understand bid/ask, spreads, pips, lots. Watch YouTube tutorials on candlestick basics. No trading yet — just absorb.
Days 4-7: Platform Mastery
Download a demo account (MT4, MT5, or TradingView). Learn every button, every order type. Place 50+ demo trades just to understand execution. Break things on purpose.
Days 8-14: Chart Reading
Study 100 historical charts. Mark support/resistance levels. Identify trends vs ranges. Label candlestick patterns. No trades — just observation and labeling.
Days 15-21: One Strategy
Pick ONE setup (e.g., trend pullback to support). Write clear rules for entry, stop loss, and target. Paper trade this setup 20+ times.
Days 22-28: Demo Trading
Trade your single strategy on demo with full position sizing math. Journal every trade. Calculate your win rate and average R after 30 trades minimum.
Days 29-30: Review & Decision
Analyze your journal. Are you profitable on demo? Did you follow your rules? If yes to both, consider going live with MINIMUM size. If no, repeat Days 15-28.
⚠️ CRITICAL WARNING

Do NOT fund a live account until you have:
✗ 30+ demo trades with your exact strategy
✗ Positive expectancy over those trades
✗ Proven you can follow rules without deviation
✗ Money you can lose entirely without financial hardship

This is not optional. This is survival.

How to Read Charts Like a Pro

Charts are the language of markets. Learn to read them fluently before trading.

📊 Anatomy of a Candlestick
Open: Where price started in this period
Close: Where price ended (most important)
High: Maximum price reached
Low: Minimum price reached
Body: Distance between open and close
Wicks: Price rejection areas (key information)
📈 What Candlesticks Tell You
Big Green Body

Strong buying pressure. Buyers dominated the entire session. Bullish momentum.

Big Red Body

Strong selling pressure. Sellers dominated the entire session. Bearish momentum.

📍 Long Lower Wick

Price went down but buyers pushed it back up. Rejection of lower prices. Potential support.

📍 Long Upper Wick

Price went up but sellers pushed it back down. Rejection of higher prices. Potential resistance.

🎯 Support & Resistance — The Foundation of All Trading
SUPPORT (Green Line)

A price level where buying interest is strong enough to prevent further decline. Think of it as a "floor." When price approaches support, expect bounces — until it breaks.

RESISTANCE (Red Line)

A price level where selling interest is strong enough to prevent further advance. Think of it as a "ceiling." When price approaches resistance, expect rejections — until it breaks.

KEY CONCEPT: When support breaks, it becomes resistance. When resistance breaks, it becomes support. This "flip" is one of the highest-probability trade setups.
📈 Trend Structure — Higher Highs, Higher Lows
🟢 UPTREND
  • Higher Highs (HH) — Each peak above previous
  • Higher Lows (HL) — Each dip above previous dip
  • Trade with the trend: Buy pullbacks to HL
  • Trend breaks when price makes Lower Low
🔴 DOWNTREND
  • Lower Highs (LH) — Each rally below previous
  • Lower Lows (LL) — Each drop below previous
  • Trade with the trend: Sell rallies to LH
  • Trend breaks when price makes Higher High

Macro Patterns
Classic Chart Patterns

Multi-candle formations that play out over days to weeks. Higher timeframes = higher reliability.

Head & Shoulders
🔴 Bearish Reversal
SELL signal: Short break of neckline + retest fails. Stop above right shoulder. Target = head height projected down.

Needs time separation between peaks. Volume often lower on right shoulder. Classic top reversal after uptrend.

Inverse Head & Shoulders
🟢 Bullish Reversal
BUY signal: Long on neckline breakout + retest holds. Stop below right shoulder. Target = head depth projected up.
Double Top
🔴 Bearish Reversal
SELL signal: Two equal highs + break of neckline. Second peak ideally has less volume. Stop above tops.
Bull Flag / Pennant
🟢 Bullish Continuation
BUY signal: Strong pole + tight consolidation + breakout above flag boundary. Stop inside flag. Target = pole height added to breakout.
Ascending Triangle
🟢 Bullish Continuation
BUY signal: Flat resistance + rising support. Enter on breakout above resistance. Target = triangle height.
Descending Triangle
🔴 Bearish Continuation
SELL signal: Flat support + falling resistance. Short on breakdown below support. Target = triangle height.
Playbooks
Complete Trading Strategies

Choose one. Master it before adding another. Context → Location → Trigger → Risk is the universal template.

Trend Following

Trade pullbacks in the direction of higher-timeframe bias. Never fight strong trends — join them on weakness.

Entry Conditions (all must be true):
① HTF (daily/weekly) shows clear HH/HL structure
② Price pulls back to S/R or moving average
③ Trigger candle appears (hammer, engulfing, pinbar)
④ RSI not at extreme (avoid chasing)
BUY: Pullback to 50MA + bullish engulfing at support SELL: Rally to resistance + bearish engulfing rejection AVOID: Sideways chop with ADX below 20
Trade Template
SetupHTF uptrend, LTF pullback to level
EntryBreak of trigger candle high (+1 tick)
StopBelow pattern low + spread
Target 11.5R — scale 50% out
Target 22.5R — trail remainder
InvalidationClose below stop, no adjustment
Best marketsFX majors, trending stocks, futures
AvoidNews events, pre-earnings, thin markets
Mean Reversion

Fade stretched moves back to equilibrium. Only valid in range-bound conditions — the single most misused strategy.

Entry Conditions:
① ADX below 20 (ranging market confirmed)
② RSI at 70+ (sell) or 30- (buy) extreme
③ Price at defined S/R boundary
④ Reversal trigger candle present
BUY: RSI < 30 + hammer at range support SELL: RSI > 70 + shooting star at range resistance AVOID: Any confirmed trending market (ADX > 25)
Trade Template
FilterADX < 20, defined range for 10+ bars
EntryLimit at boundary or break of reversal candle
StopOutside range (beyond swing high/low)
TargetMidline of range, then opposite side
Time stopExit if no movement in 3-5 candles
Best marketsFX during Asian session, sector ETFs
AvoidTrending breakouts, news environments
Breakout / Retest

Trade acceptance outside a level — not the first tick through. Waiting for retest drastically improves win rate.

Entry Conditions:
① Clear consolidation / accumulation zone
② Price breaks and closes beyond level
③ Wait for retest of broken level
④ Retest holds + trigger candle forms
BUY: Break above resistance, retest holds as support SELL: Break below support, retest fails at that level AVOID: Breakouts into major counter-trend levels
Trade Template
EntryLimit at retest level (reactive entry)
Aggressive entryMarket on breakout close (momentum)
StopBeyond failed retest candle low/high
TargetNext structural level or 2R minimum
Volume checkBreak should have above-avg volume (stocks)
Best marketsStocks, crypto, index futures
False breakout?Price reclaims level — exit immediately
Scalping

High frequency, tiny targets. Costs dominate. Not beginner-friendly — requires fast execution and strict rules.

Warning: Spread + commission as % of target is often 30-60% for scalpers. You MUST measure this BEFORE trading. Most retail brokers are not suitable for scalping.
BUY: Support bounce + DOM shows buyers absorbing SELL: Resistance rejection + speed increases on down move REQUIRED: Spread must be <15% of target

Swing Trading

Hold days to weeks. Align daily trigger with weekly structure. Overnight risk is real — size accordingly.

Multi-Timeframe Approach:

Daily: Find S/R levels, pattern setup
4H: Time entry, find trigger candle
Entry: Execute with daily ATR-based stop
BUY: Weekly uptrend + daily pullback to S + 4H hammer SELL: Weekly downtrend + daily bounce to R + 4H shooting star SIZE: Use 0.5-1% risk — overnight gaps are real

Supply & Demand Zones

Trade from areas where institutional orders were placed. Look for the "origin" of strong moves — that's where unfilled orders remain.

Zone Identification:
① Find strong impulse move (3+ big candles)
② Mark the base before the move (1-3 candles)
③ Zone = High to low of that base
④ Fresh zones (untested) are strongest
BUY: Price returns to demand zone + bullish trigger SELL: Price returns to supply zone + bearish trigger SKIP: Zones tested 2+ times (orders likely filled)
Trade Template
Zone qualityStrong departure, fresh, in trend direction
EntryLimit at zone edge or trigger candle
StopBeyond opposite zone edge
TargetOpposite zone or 2R minimum
Time decayOld zones (30+ bars) lose strength
Best marketsAny, works especially well on indices
Combine withTrend direction, Fibonacci levels
Fibonacci Retracement

Use mathematical ratios to find probable reversal zones. 38.2%, 50%, and 61.8% are the key levels.

How to Draw Fibs:
① Identify clear swing high and swing low
② In uptrend: draw from low to high
③ In downtrend: draw from high to low
④ Watch for confluence with S/R levels
BUY: Pullback to 61.8% + support + trigger candle SELL: Rally to 61.8% + resistance + trigger candle GOLDEN ZONE: 50%-61.8% = highest probability
Key Fibonacci Levels
23.6%Shallow — strong trend continuation
38.2%Normal pullback in strong trend
50.0%Psychological level (not true Fib)
61.8%Golden ratio — high probability reversal
78.6%Deep retracement — trend may be failing
Extensions127.2%, 161.8% for profit targets
ConfluenceFib + S/R + pattern = best setups
Technical Tools
Indicators — How Pros Use Them

Indicators are derivatives of price. Use them to confirm structure, not replace it. One primary + one volatility indicator is enough.

📊 RSI (Relative Strength Index)
RSI = 100 - (100 / (1 + RS))

Measures momentum. In trends, RSI can stay "extreme" for long periods — don't fade it alone.

BUY: <30 + divergence + support candle SELL: >70 + divergence + resistance candle WAIT: 40-60 zone with no clear structure
📉 MACD
MACD = EMA(12) - EMA(26)

Trend/momentum hybrid. Histogram shows momentum decay — great for exit management, not entries alone.

BUY: Signal line cross up + uptrend structure SELL: Signal line cross down + downtrend AVOID: Crossovers in sideways markets = whipsaw
📏 ATR (Average True Range)
ATR = avg(max(H-L, |H-Prev C|, |L-Prev C|))

Baseline volatility. Use for stop placement (1.5-2× ATR), position sizing, and detecting volatility regime changes.

STOP = Entry - (1.5 × ATR) SIZE = Account Risk $ / (ATR × Point Value) LOW ATR = Quiet market, expect expansion soon
〰️ Bollinger Bands
Upper = SMA + (2 × StdDev)

Volatility envelope. Squeeze = volatility contraction before expansion. Touch ≠ automatic reversal.

BREAK: Squeeze breakout with volume FADE: Band touch + reversal candle + ranging WALKING THE BAND = trending, don't fade!
📈 Moving Averages
EMA = Price × k + EMA_prev × (1-k)

Trend filter. Price vs 50/200 MA = macro regime. Use slope and location vs structure, not just crossovers.

BUY ZONE: Price above rising 50MA SELL ZONE: Price below falling 50MA FLAT MA = Range market, reduce size
📊 Volume
Volume = Units traded in period

Validates breakouts on exchange-traded assets. Volume precedes price. High volume = conviction.

CONFIRM: Breakout + volume >150% avg WARN: New high + declining volume LOW VOLUME: Avoid new entries
🎯 ADX (Trend Strength)
ADX = Smoothed DI movement

Measures trend strength, not direction. ADX > 25 = trending. ADX < 20 = ranging. Essential filter.

TREND: ADX > 25 = use trend strategies RANGE: ADX < 20 = use mean reversion RISING ADX = Trend strengthening
📐 Stochastic
%K = (C-L14)/(H14-L14) × 100

Momentum oscillator comparing close to range. Best in ranges. In trends, can stay overbought/oversold.

BUY: %K < 20 + cross up + support SELL: %K > 80 + cross down + resistance DIVERGENCE: Most reliable signal
🔄 VWAP
VWAP = Σ(Price × Volume) / ΣVolume

Volume-weighted average price. Institutional benchmark. Intraday traders use as dynamic S/R.

BUY: Price reclaims VWAP from below SELL: Price rejects VWAP from above RESETS daily — intraday tool only
⚠️ Indicator Reality Check: More indicators ≠ better trading. In fact, analysis paralysis from indicator overload is one of the top reasons traders fail. Pick 2-3 maximum. Master them. Ignore the rest.

Top 12 Beginner Mistakes

Every one of these costs real money. Learn them here for free instead of paying the market to teach you.

❌ No Stop Loss
Entering trades without a predetermined exit point. "I'll just watch it" always ends badly.
✅ FIX: Set stop loss BEFORE entry. Use ATR-based or structure-based stops. No exceptions.
❌ Risking Too Much
Putting 10-20% of account on one trade. One bad trade can wipe weeks of gains.
✅ FIX: Risk 1% max per trade. After 10 losses (unlikely), you still have 90% of capital.
❌ Overtrading
Taking 20+ trades per day out of boredom or need for action. Most are low-quality.
✅ FIX: Set a max trades/day limit (3-5). Quality over quantity. No trade = valid decision.
❌ Revenge Trading
Immediately re-entering after a loss to "make it back." Emotions destroy accounts.
✅ FIX: Mandatory 30-min break after any loss. 2 losses = done for the day.
❌ Moving Stop Losses
Widening stops to avoid getting stopped out. Turns small loss into catastrophic loss.
✅ FIX: Stop is set at entry. Only move it to lock in profits, NEVER to avoid loss.
❌ Chasing Trades
Entering late after price has already moved significantly. FOMO buys tops, sells bottoms.
✅ FIX: Wait for pullbacks. If you missed it, you missed it. There's always another trade.
❌ No Trading Plan
Making decisions in the moment based on feelings. Inconsistent results guaranteed.
✅ FIX: Written plan: setups, entries, exits, risk rules. Trade the plan, review weekly.
❌ Indicator Overload
10 indicators on chart = analysis paralysis. Conflicting signals, no action, or random action.
✅ FIX: Price action + one trend indicator + one volatility indicator. Maximum 3.
❌ Averaging Down
Adding to losing positions hoping for recovery. Increases risk when you should be reducing it.
✅ FIX: Never add to losers. Only add to winners (scaling in) with predefined rules.
❌ Trading News Blind
Being in positions during major announcements without awareness. Gaps and slippage destroy.
✅ FIX: Check economic calendar daily. Reduce size or close before high-impact events.
❌ Ignoring Correlation
Long EUR/USD, GBP/USD, and AUD/USD = essentially one big USD short with 3x risk.
✅ FIX: Understand correlations. Limit exposure to any single theme/currency/sector.
❌ Skipping Demo
Going live before proving profitability on demo. Paying tuition to the market unnecessarily.
✅ FIX: 30+ demo trades with positive expectancy before ANY live trading. Non-negotiable.

📋 Post-Trade Review (every single trade)
  1. Did price action after exit validate or invalidate my thesis?

Frequently Asked Questions
How much money do I need to start trading?

You only need enough to properly size positions with 0.5–1% risk per trade. For forex with a $1,000 account and 20-pip stop: you'd need micro lots. More important than account size is trading money you can lose entirely — it's tuition. Paper trade until your process is consistent first.

What win rate do I need to be profitable?

Win rate alone is meaningless. A 40% win rate can be very profitable if average wins are 3× average losses. Track Expectancy = (Win% × Avg Win R) − (Loss% × Avg Loss R). Target positive expectancy over 30+ trades. Focus on R-multiples, not win rate.

Should I buy or sell this signal?

No one can tell you this reliably. Signals work in context: the same candle pattern at support in an uptrend is very different from the same pattern in a downtrend at resistance. Learn the framework: Context (HTF trend) → Location (S/R) → Trigger (pattern) → Risk (stop/size).

How long until I'm consistently profitable?

Budget 1–3 years for most traders working at it seriously. The learning curve has phases: platform → risk → one setup → process → psychology → scaling. Most failures happen because people try to skip phases.

Are paid signal services worth it?

If you don't understand why a signal fires, you can't manage the trade when it goes against you. The most important skill is reading your own charts. Use third-party content for education only, never as a replacement for your own process.

What timeframe should I trade?

Start with the daily chart — it filters noise and each bar takes 24 hours to form, forcing patience. Once your setup works on daily, learn the 4H for entries. Never start with tick charts or 1-min — you'll overtrade and over-optimize.

Why do patterns sometimes fail?

Because markets are probabilistic, not deterministic. Patterns fail when: (1) context is wrong (counter-trend), (2) at wrong location (middle of range, not S/R), (3) confirmation never happened, or (4) a larger force (news, big player) overrides the pattern.

Is trading gambling?

Without an edge, yes. With a tested strategy, proper risk management, and disciplined execution, trading is a probability game with positive expected value — like being the house in a casino, not a gambler. The difference is in your approach.

Trading Glossary
Bid / Ask
Bid = buyers' price. Ask = sellers' price. You buy at ask, sell at bid. The gap is the spread — your immediate cost.
R-Multiple
Result expressed in units of initial risk. +2R means you made twice your risk. Track all trades in R to normalize different position sizes.
Expectancy
Average R per trade across a large sample. Positive expectancy = system has edge after costs.
Drawdown
Peak-to-trough decline in account equity. Max drawdown tells you the worst historical loss streak.
ATR
Average True Range — average daily (or period) volatility. Used to set stops and normalize position sizing.
Higher Timeframe (HTF)
The chart above your trading timeframe. Always trade with HTF bias — counter-trend trades require skill.
Support / Resistance
Price levels where buyers (support) or sellers (resistance) previously entered in force.
HH / HL / LH / LL
Higher High/Higher Low = uptrend. Lower High/Lower Low = downtrend. Three consecutive HH/HL confirms a trend.
Pip
Price Interest Point — smallest standard price move in FX. EUR/USD: 0.0001 = 1 pip.
Lot Size
Standard lot = 100,000 units. Mini = 10,000. Micro = 1,000. Start with micros.
Margin / Leverage
Margin is collateral. Leverage amplifies gains AND losses equally — it does not improve your edge.
Slippage
Difference between expected and actual execution price. Common during news or low liquidity.
Liquidity
How easily you can enter/exit without moving price. High liquidity = tight spreads, fast fills.
Volatility
How much price moves. High volatility = bigger swings, wider stops needed, more opportunity and risk.
Confluence
Multiple factors aligning at one price. S/R + pattern + Fib + MA = high confluence = higher probability.
Divergence
Price makes new high/low but indicator doesn't confirm. Signals potential reversal.
